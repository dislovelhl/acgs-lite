## The Problem Nobody Is Solving

$203 billion was invested in building AI decision engines in 2025. Less than 1% went into the infrastructure to constrain them.

A single mother applies for a mortgage. 742 credit score. Stable employment. The AI rejects her in 340 milliseconds. No human review. No appeal. No audit trail.

This is not a hypothetical. It is the state of AI decision-making today. The models are powerful. The governance is missing.

Guardrails filter unsafe outputs. ACGS governs actions.

## What Is ACGS?

ACGS is an open-source constitutional governance layer for AI agents. Think of it as a constitution for your AI system -- machine-readable rules that are validated at runtime, before the agent acts.

```bash
pip install acgs-lite
```

```python
from acgs_lite import Constitution, GovernedAgent

constitution = Constitution.from_yaml("rules.yaml")
agent = GovernedAgent(my_agent, constitution=constitution)
result = agent.run("process this loan application")
```

Four lines. Your agent is now governed. Every action validated. Every decision audited. Violations blocked before they execute.

**Key capabilities:**

- **Constitutional rules** defined in YAML, enforced at runtime
- **MACI separation of powers** -- the AI that proposes a decision cannot be the AI that approves it
- **Tamper-evident audit trail** -- every governance decision recorded in verifiable JSONL
- **EU AI Act compliance** -- `acgs-lite eu-ai-act --domain healthcare` generates a full assessment
- **HIPAA compliance** -- built-in checklist for healthcare AI
- **Constrained decoding** -- force LLM outputs to conform to governance schemas
- **CLI tools** -- `acgs-lite init`, `acgs-lite test`, `acgs-lite observe`
