# Budget Creation Playbook

**Audience:** Founder (pre-sales), future sales team
**Purpose:** How to get ACGS purchased when "AI governance" is not an existing budget line item
**Date:** 2026-03-19

---

## The Core Problem

"AI governance" is a new category. Most organizations in 2026 do not have a budget line item for it. Selling ACGS requires **attaching to an existing budget** — not creating a new one.

**Rule:** Never sell "AI governance." Always sell into an existing need.

## Deal Acceleration Tactics

### 1. The Audit Gap Letter

Prepare a one-page "AI Governance Gap Assessment" for the prospect:
- List their AI systems (ask in discovery)
- Map each to applicable regulatory frameworks
- Highlight gaps (no audit trail, no separation of duties, no compliance reporting)
- Show ACGS coverage for each gap
- Close with: "This gap assessment took 15 minutes. Closing these gaps with ACGS takes 5 minutes more."

### 2. The CFO Calculator

Simple ROI calculation:
```
Annual cost of ACGS Pro:           $3,588
Annual cost of one compliance audit finding:  $15,000-50,000
Annual cost of one lost enterprise deal:      $50,000-500,000
Annual cost of EU AI Act minimum fine:        EUR 7,500,000
ROI: ACGS pays for itself if it prevents ONE audit finding.
```

### 3. The "Start Free, Upgrade When Needed" Path

Remove all purchase friction:
1. Champion installs free tier (zero approval needed)
2. Team uses it for 2-4 weeks (habit formation)
3. Audit/compliance event creates urgency
4. Champion requests Pro upgrade with existing evidence of value
5. Budget owner approves based on demonstrated need, not speculative value

**This is the primary conversion path.** Do not try to sell Pro on first contact. Let the free tier create the internal champion.
