# ACGS Pitch Deck Outline

**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Audience:** investors, design partners, strategic customers
**Purpose:** narrative structure for a 10-slide founder deck

---

## Slide 1: Title

**Title:** ACGS  
**Subtitle:** Constitutional governance for AI agents

**Key line:**  
AI agents can take actions. They need a constitution.

---

## Slide 2: The Shift

**Title:** AI is moving from answers to actions

**Points**
- Models no longer only generate text
- Agents can now call tools, approve workflows, and trigger real-world changes
- That changes the control problem

**Speaker note**  
The market has spent two years optimizing model capability. Governance has not caught up to agent behavior.

---

## Slide 3: The Gap

**Title:** The AI stack still lacks a runtime governance layer

**Points**
- Orchestration frameworks manage flow
- Guardrails tools manage prompts and outputs
- Governance platforms manage oversight and policy workflow
- None cleanly own governance inside execution

**Key line:**  
Prompts are not governance. Dashboards are not runtime control.

---

## Slide 4: Why Existing Categories Fall Short

**Title:** Current categories solve adjacent problems

**Points**
- GRC platforms govern from above
- Guardrails products focus on content and safety
- Policy engines handle general policy-as-code
- Agent frameworks orchestrate but do not govern

**Key line:**  
The missing layer is the one that decides whether an agent may act.

---

## Slide 5: What ACGS Is

**Title:** A constitutional governance layer for agentic systems

**Points**
- Machine-readable constitutions
- Runtime action evaluation
- Structural proposer / validator / executor separation
- Audit-ready evidence tied to rule state

**Key line:**  
Guardrails filter outputs. ACGS governs actions.

## Slide 7: Why It Matters in Regulated AI

**Title:** Governance moves from optional to required

**Points**
- EU AI Act and related governance expectations raise the bar
- Buyers need evidence, not intent
- Agentic workflows multiply approval, accountability, and audit problems

**Key line:**  
Once AI can act, the question becomes institutional: who approved what, under which rules?

---

## Slide 8: Competitive Position

**Title:** ACGS occupies the missing layer

**Points**
- Credo / Holistic / FairNow: governance oversight
- NeMo / Guardrails / Lakera / Arthur: safety and runtime protection
- OPA: policy infrastructure
- LangGraph: orchestration
- ACGS: runtime constitutional governance

**Key line:**  
Credo governs from above. NeMo guards content. LangGraph orchestrates flow. ACGS governs what the agent is allowed to do.

---

## Slide 9: Go-To-Market

**Title:** Developer-first, compliance-out

**Points**
- Start with engineers integrating runtime governance
- Deliver outputs compliance and security teams can consume
- Wedge through Git-native governance, regulated AI, and agent platforms

**Key line:**  
Adopt like infrastructure. Consume like governance.

---

## Slide 10: Closing Thesis

**Title:** A new control plane for the agent era

**Points**
- AI capability is accelerating faster than AI governance
- The next layer of the stack is not more generation, but more control
- ACGS is building the constitutional layer for that stack

**Closing line:**  
If agents can act, they need a constitution.
