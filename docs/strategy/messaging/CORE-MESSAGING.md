## Core Positioning

ACGS is the constitutional governance layer for AI agents.

It should be positioned as **runtime constitutional governance for agentic systems**.

It should not be positioned primarily as:
- a generic AI governance dashboard
- a generic LLM guardrails toolkit
- a generic agent orchestration framework

---

- **Guardrails filter outputs. ACGS governs actions.**

---

## Expanded Description

ACGS embeds governance directly into agent runtime. Before an agent can approve, deny, deploy,
escalate, or execute, ACGS can evaluate machine-readable rules, enforce role boundaries, and
record what happened against a specific governance state.

That makes ACGS a runtime enforcement layer, not just a policy document, safety filter, or
post-hoc governance dashboard.

---

## Category Framing

### What existing categories do

- **GRC / governance platforms** organize oversight, inventory, and compliance workflows
- **Guardrails / safety tools** filter prompts, outputs, and unsafe content
- **Policy-as-code tools** evaluate general infrastructure and application policy
- **Agent frameworks** orchestrate execution and state

### What ACGS adds

ACGS governs what an agent is allowed to do, who is allowed to validate it, and what evidence is
left behind when the decision is made.

---

## Why ACGS Is Different

- **Governance happens inline at runtime** rather than only after the fact
- **Self-validation can be prevented by architecture** via explicit role separation
- **Decisions can be tied to explicit rule state** instead of generic logs alone
- **Audit evidence is part of the product** rather than a reporting afterthought
- **Developers can integrate quickly** while compliance and security teams can consume the output

---

## Founder Narrative

Most AI infrastructure helps agents do more.

Almost none of it constrains what those agents are allowed to do once they can take actions in the
world.

That is the gap ACGS is built for.

The market already has tools for orchestration, observability, guardrails, and compliance
dashboards. What it lacks is a constitutional layer inside the runtime: the system that decides
whether an agent may act, who may approve it, and how that governance can later be proven.

ACGS exists to make governance executable.
