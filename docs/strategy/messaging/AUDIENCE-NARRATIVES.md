# ACGS Audience-Segmented Messaging

**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Updated focus:** runtime constitutional governance for agentic systems

---

## Core Positioning (All Audiences)

**One-liner:** ACGS is the constitutional governance layer for AI agents.

**Short line:** Guardrails filter outputs. ACGS governs actions.

**Tagline:** If agents can act, they need a constitution.

---

## Audience 1: Developers

### Core Message

> **Add runtime governance to your agents.** You already lint code and scan for vulnerabilities.
> ACGS lets you wrap agents with machine-readable constitutional rules, keep proposer and
> validator roles separate, and leave behind an audit trail your team can inspect.

### Lead With

- `pip install acgs-lite`
- fast local integration
- machine-readable rules
- governed actions inside the runtime

### Good Content Angles

- "Adding runtime governance to your FastAPI agent in 5 minutes"
- "How ACGS evaluates constitutional rules on the Rust hot path"
- "Why AI agents should not validate their own output"
- "Building a GitLab governance gate in YAML"

### What Not To Say

- Don't lead with analyst reports or enterprise procurement language
- Don't oversell compliance before showing code
- Don't force the founder story into technical copy

---

## Audience 2: CTOs / VP Engineering

### Core Message

> **Governance infrastructure, not governance theater.** ACGS gives engineering a runtime control
> plane for agentic systems and gives risk teams evidence they can review.

### Lead With

- architectural fit
- runtime control
- explicit decision boundaries
- evidence generation

### Good Content Angles

- "Why AI agents need separation of powers"
- "From policy documents to governed runtime actions"
- "ACGS architecture: constitutional state, audit trails, and runtime enforcement"

### What Not To Say

- Don't claim perfect coverage
- Don't flatten the architecture into a simple feature list
- Don't sell it like a dashboard product

## Audience 3: CISOs / DPOs / Compliance Leaders

### Core Message

> **Compliance evidence, not compliance promises.** ACGS helps turn governance from policy
> documents into enforceable runtime controls and reviewable evidence.

### Lead With

- reviewable evidence
- separation of duties
- traceability to rule state
- framework-aligned outputs

### Good Content Angles

- "What runtime governance evidence looks like under the EU AI Act"
- "What your auditor will ask about AI agents"
- "Why AI systems need a constitutional layer"
- "Separation of duties for agentic systems"

### What Not To Say

- Don't say the tool alone makes a system compliant
- Don't say "open source" without also explaining the commercial path
- Don't ignore operational risk questions

---

## Audience 4: Investors

### Core Message

> **Constitutional governance for AI is an infrastructure layer the stack still lacks.** The
> market has orchestration, observability, and safety controls, but not a clear runtime governance
> plane for agentic systems.

### Lead With

- category gap
- structural moat
- developer-first adoption
- compliance-out outputs

### Pitch Structure

1. AI is moving from answers to actions
2. Existing categories do not own runtime governance
3. ACGS is the constitutional layer inside execution
4. Product primitives: rules, separation, audit, evidence
5. GTM: developer-first, compliance-out

### What Not To Say

- Don't lead with the founder story before the category thesis
- Don't claim growth without bottoms-up logic
- Don't hide concentration risk

---

## Audience 5: Design Partners

### Core Message

> **Shape the future of AI runtime governance.** We are looking for a small set of design partners
> building serious agentic systems in regulated or risk-sensitive environments.

### Offer

- direct founder access
- priority feature input
- fast feedback loop on integrations and workflows
- early influence over governed action patterns

### What Good Partners Look Like

- regulated or compliance-heavy environment
- active agent or copilot deployment
- engineering team willing to integrate early
- buyer who cares about runtime controls, not only policy docs
