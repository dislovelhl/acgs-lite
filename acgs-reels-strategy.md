# ACGS Reels Strategy Pack

Date: 2026-03-19

## Core Messaging

- Agents should not validate their own outputs.
- Separation of proposer, validator, executor.
- Constitutional validation before action.
- 560ns median local validation.
- Governance trail for enterprise systems.

## Strategic Positioning

Do not lead with:
- "AI going rogue"
- generic "AI safety"
- "compliant instantly"
- "without slowing development" without proof

Lead with:
- architectural failure: agents grading their own work
- governed execution
- fast local validation
- accountable decision trails

## ACGS Angle Pack

### Angle 1: Fear / Risk

Hook options:
1. The scary part of AI agents is not intelligence. It is unauthorized action.
2. Most AI agent demos look impressive until you ask who approves the decision.
3. If your agent can act without governance, you do not have autonomy. You have liability.

Script:

```text
Everybody wants autonomous agents until the agent makes a decision nobody approved.
That is the real problem.
Not “can it reason?”
Can it stay inside policy, role boundaries, and actual governance?
ACGS is built for that layer.
It separates proposer, validator, and executor roles so an agent does not approve its own output.
It adds constitutional validation before action.
So instead of trusting vibes, you can enforce what the system is allowed to do.
If you are building agents for real environments, this is the part you cannot skip.
```

First-frame text:

`AI agents are not scary because they think. They’re scary because they act.`

Caption:

`The hard part of AI agents is not capability. It is control.`

### Angle 2: Speed / Tech

Hook options:
1. We are validating AI actions in 560 nanoseconds median latency.
2. Governance usually sounds slow until you measure it.
3. If your safety layer kills performance, developers will route around it.

Script:

```text
Most people hear “governance layer” and assume it is going to slow everything down.
That is exactly why most teams resist adding one.
But ACGS validates actions at 560 nanoseconds median latency.
That matters because safety only works when developers can afford to keep it on.
No giant delay. No excuse to bypass the control plane.
You can validate agent actions fast enough that governance stops being the bottleneck.
That is when safety gets real.
Not when it looks good in a diagram.
When it survives production constraints.
```

First-frame text:

`Fast enough to keep governance in the loop.`

If you are serious about AI in regulated environments, this is the layer to pay attention to.

## Production Guidance

For broader emotional resonance:
- use trust
- use liability
- use control
- use relief

Do not use:
- "single mom" framing for ACGS
- manipulative identity borrowing
- vague fear language without architecture detail

Use instead:
- "Would you let this system act without review?"
- "Who owns the decision when the agent is wrong?"
- "Can you prove why the system acted?"
- "What if governance did not slow the stack down?"
